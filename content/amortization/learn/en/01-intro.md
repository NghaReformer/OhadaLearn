# Loan Amortization — Introduction

## What Is Amortization?

**Loan amortization** is the process of paying off a debt through a sequence of scheduled payments. Each payment covers two things: the **interest** accrued on the outstanding balance since the last payment, and a **principal repayment** that reduces that balance. Over the life of the loan, interest shrinks and principal grows — the mechanics differ by method.

## Why It Matters

Understanding amortization answers three practical questions every finance professional faces:

1. **How much will I pay each period?** — the periodic cash commitment.
2. **What is the true cost of credit?** — total interest, APR, effective annual rate.
3. **How should I record the loan?** — the journal entries that flow through the balance sheet and income statement.

## Methods at a Glance

- **Annuity (French)** — constant total payment; interest front-loaded.
- **Linear (Constant Principal)** — equal principal each period; payments decline.
- **Bullet (In Fine)** — interest-only during the term, principal at maturity.
- **Progressive** — payment grows by a fixed rate each period (indexed loans).
- **Balloon** — annuity payments with a lump principal at a chosen period.
