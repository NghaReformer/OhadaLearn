# Normal Balance Rules

## What Is a Normal Balance?

Every account has a **normal balance** — the side (debit or credit) that *increases* the account. Recording on the opposite side *decreases* it.

## The Rules

### Debit-Normal Accounts (increase with debits)

- **Assets** (Classes 2, 3, 5 and asset accounts in 4) — you *debit* to increase
- **Expenses** (Classes 6, 8 charges) — you *debit* to increase

### Credit-Normal Accounts (increase with credits)

- **Liabilities** (Class 1 liabilities, liability accounts in 4) — you *credit* to increase
- **Equity** (Class 1 equity) — you *credit* to increase
- **Revenue** (Classes 7, 8 products) — you *credit* to increase

## Quick Reference

| Account Type | Normal Balance | To Increase | To Decrease |
|-------------|---------------|-------------|-------------|
| Asset | Debit | Debit | Credit |
| Expense | Debit | Debit | Credit |
| Liability | Credit | Credit | Debit |
| Equity | Credit | Credit | Debit |
| Revenue | Credit | Credit | Debit |

## Contra Accounts

Some accounts have the *opposite* normal balance of their category. For example:
- **Accumulated depreciation** (`28xx`) is an asset account but has a **credit** normal balance — it reduces the gross asset value
- **Inventory provisions** (`39`) also carry a credit balance

## Memory Aid

Think of the accounting equation expanded:

$$A + E_{xp} = L + Eq + R_{ev}$$

Everything on the **left** of the equation is debit-normal. Everything on the **right** is credit-normal.
