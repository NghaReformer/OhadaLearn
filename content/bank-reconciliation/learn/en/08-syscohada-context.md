# SYSCOHADA Context

## The OHADA Zone

The Organisation for the Harmonisation of Business Law in Africa (OHADA) covers 17 francophone African states. The **SYSCOHADA** chart of accounts (most recently revised in 2018, effective 2019) is the mandatory accounting framework across the zone.

For bank reconciliation, the relevant SYSCOHADA accounts are:

| Code | Name | Used for |
|---|---|---|
| 411 | Clients | NSF customer checks |
| 472 | Comptes d'attente | Suspense pending reclassification |
| 521 | Banque | The bank account being reconciled |
| 627 | Services bancaires et assimilés | Bank service charges |
| 771 | Intérêts reçus | Interest income |

## The Two Systems: Normal & Minimal

SYSCOHADA defines two reporting systems:

- **Système Normal** — used by entities above turnover thresholds. Requires a complete set of statements including the Tableau des Flux de Trésorerie (cash-flow statement).
- **Système Minimal de Trésorerie (SMT)** — for very small entities. Cash-basis accounting only, with simplified records.

Bank reconciliation is required under both systems. Under SMT it is *the* primary control on cash; under Système Normal it feeds the cash-flow statement and supports the audit opinion.

## Internal Control Expectation

OHADA's audit guidance considers monthly bank reconciliation a baseline control. Auditors will:

1. Verify reconciliations are performed monthly within an acceptable lag.
2. Examine reconciling items older than 60 days for evidence of follow-up.
3. Re-perform a sample of reconciliations independently.

Stale outstanding checks (older than the validity period — typically 1 year) and persistent unidentified items are red flags that escalate audit risk.
