# Why We Reconcile

## Three Reasons

**Detect errors early.** Whoever owns the books is one keystroke away from a wrong amount, a swapped debit and credit, or a missing entry. The reconciliation is the routine that surfaces those mistakes within a month, not at year-end.

**Catch fraud.** Unauthorized withdrawals, kiting between two bank accounts, and lapping schemes all leave a residue that surfaces in a careful reconciliation. SYSCOHADA's internal-control framework (and any external audit) treats unreconciled bank accounts as a red flag.

**Establish the true cash.** The figure on the bank statement is *the bank's view* of your cash; the figure in the ledger is *your view*. Neither is the truth — the truth is what would be in the bank if every pending transaction settled. The reconciliation produces that truth.

## When to Reconcile

Reconcile every bank account every month, immediately after receiving the statement. For high-volume accounts, daily reconciliation is appropriate. The longer you wait, the harder it becomes to investigate stale items — and the more pressure the deadline puts on shortcuts.
