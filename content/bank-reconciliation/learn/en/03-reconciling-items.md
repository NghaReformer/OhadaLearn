# Reconciling Items

Every difference between the bank statement and the ledger falls into one of nine categories. Five hit the books; two hit the bank's records; two are pure timing.

## Adjust the Books

| Category | What it is | OHADA accounts |
|---|---|---|
| **Bank charges** | Service fees the bank debited but you have not booked | DR 627 / CR 521 |
| **Interest earned** | Interest the bank credited but you have not booked | DR 521 / CR 771 |
| **NSF check** | Customer check that bounced — reverse the deposit and re-establish the receivable | DR 411 / CR 521 |
| **Direct debit** | Authorized auto-withdrawal (utilities, insurance) on statement only | DR 62 / CR 521 |
| **Standing order** | Recurring credit on statement only | DR 521 / CR 62 |

## Adjust the Bank (flag for correction)

| Category | What it is |
|---|---|
| **Bank error** | The bank debited or credited the wrong amount, account, or party. Flag it; do **not** book it. |

## Pure Timing — No Entry Needed

| Category | What it is |
|---|---|
| **Outstanding check** | Check you wrote and recorded; bank has not cleared it yet |
| **Deposit in transit** | Deposit you recorded; bank has not posted it yet |

## Last Category: Company Errors

A **company error** is a bookkeeping mistake on your side — wrong amount, swapped sides, duplicate posting. Correct it in the books with whichever entry restores the truth. The reconciliation only tells you it exists; finding it requires reading the ledger detail.
