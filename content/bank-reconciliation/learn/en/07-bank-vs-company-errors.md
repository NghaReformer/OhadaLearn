# Bank Error vs. Company Error

## Who Acts?

The single most important classification question: **who caused the mistake?** The answer determines which side of the reconciliation receives the adjustment.

- **Bank error** → the bank must correct it. You flag it on the reconciliation and notify the bank. **No journal entry.**
- **Company error** → you correct it in your ledger. **One or more journal entries.**

## How to Tell Them Apart

Compare the bank's record of the transaction with what *should* have happened:

- Bank shows 12,500 for a check actually written for 11,500 → **bank error** (bank's data entry).
- Bank shows 11,500 for a check actually written for 11,500 but your ledger has 12,500 → **company error** (your data entry).
- Bank debits your account for a transaction belonging to another customer → **bank error**.
- You recorded a receipt from Customer A against Customer B → **company error** (within your books, no cash impact).

## The Temptation

The natural instinct is to "just fix it in the books" because that is faster. Resist — once you write a journal entry for a bank error, you have corrupted your records to match the bank's mistake. If the bank reverses its error later, you now have a double adjustment to untangle. Always push bank errors back to the bank.
