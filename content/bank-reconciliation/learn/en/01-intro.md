# Bank Reconciliation — Introduction

## What Is a Bank Reconciliation?

A **bank reconciliation** (rapprochement bancaire) compares the cash balance shown on the bank statement against the balance shown in the company's general ledger account 521 (Banque), and explains every difference. The two balances rarely match on any given date because items take time to clear and because each side records events the other has not yet seen.

The reconciliation is one of the most important monthly controls a company performs. It catches:

- bookkeeping errors before they propagate into the financial statements,
- bank errors that would otherwise reduce the company's cash,
- timing differences that need no entry but must be tracked,
- and outright fraud — missing deposits, unauthorized withdrawals, kiting schemes.

## The Output

A finished reconciliation produces three deliverables:

1. **The reconciliation statement** (état de rapprochement) — a two-column document showing how each side moves from its raw closing balance to the same true cash figure.
2. **Adjusting journal entries** for every item the books need to record (bank charges, interest, NSF checks, errors).
3. **A flag list** of items the bank needs to correct (bank errors, unauthorized debits).

If the two adjusted balances do not equal — to the cent — the reconciliation is not complete.
