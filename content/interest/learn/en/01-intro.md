# Interest — Introduction

## A Single Idea, Three Levels

Interest is the price of money over time. Every financial transaction in the OHADA zone — a savings deposit, a commercial loan, a corporate bond — hinges on one question: *how much is a claim on a future cash flow worth today, and how much does it grow between now and then?*

This playground teaches the answer in three levels that build on each other:

1. **Simple interest** — the starter case. Interest accrues at a flat rate on the original principal. Short-term commercial paper and some SYSCOHADA trade-finance instruments still use this convention.
2. **Compound interest** — the real world. Interest earned each period becomes part of the principal for the next period. The growth curve bends upward.
3. **Effective vs nominal rate** — the crucial subtlety. A "12% nominal rate compounded monthly" is not 12%. It is 12.68%. Under SYSCOHADA Revised and IFRS 9, all financial instruments are measured at amortised cost using this effective rate.

## Why a Single Playground

Students who learn these three topics as separate chapters end up with three disconnected formulas. Students who learn them as one continuous idea end up with intuition. The effective-interest-rate formula stops being a trick to memorise and becomes the inevitable result of shortening the compounding period.

By the time you finish Mode 3, you should be able to explain why `(1 + r/m)^m − 1` is the right formula, without looking at a textbook.

## How to Use This Playground

Start in **Simple** mode. Set a principal, a rate, and a term. Watch the chart. Then switch to **Compound** and see what changes. Pin snapshots from each mode to the **Comparison drawer** on the right — that side-by-side view is where the lesson clicks.
