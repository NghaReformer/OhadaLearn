# SYSCOHADA Revised & IFRS 9 — Regional Context

## The Reform

In January 2018, the OHADA zone adopted **SYSCOHADA Revised** (the "Système Comptable OHADA" as updated in 2017). The reform aligned OHADA accounting rules with a subset of IFRS — most importantly, IFRS 9 *Financial Instruments*. Before the reform, financial instruments were measured largely at historical cost with straight-line amortisation. After the reform, they are measured at **amortised cost** using the **effective interest rate** method.

For practitioners in Cameroon, Côte d'Ivoire, Senegal, Gabon and the other 13 OHADA member states, this was the single largest change in day-to-day accounting practice since the original SYSCOHADA of 2001.

## What Changed in Practice

Before 2018:

- A 5-year bond issued at a discount would amortise that discount straight-line
- The carrying amount in the balance sheet grew linearly toward face
- Interest expense (or income, for holders) was a simple average

After 2018:

- The discount amortises on an EIR basis — the interest expense recorded equals the market rate at issuance applied to the opening carrying amount
- The carrying amount curves upward, accelerating toward face
- The "interest on financial debt" line in the income statement is no longer a flat average

This matters most for:

- Long-dated bonds (where the distortion is largest)
- Trade receivables with significant financing components (factoring arrangements, deferred-settlement contracts)
- Intra-group loans at off-market rates

## The Transition Entry

Companies that had legacy instruments on their books at 1 January 2018 were required to **restate them on transition**. The difference between the straight-line carrying amount and the EIR carrying amount at that date was posted as a one-off adjustment to retained earnings (account 11 under SYSCOHADA).

For a mid-term discount bond with 2 years elapsed out of 5, the transition adjustment is typically a *reduction* in retained earnings (because the EIR carrying amount in the early years is *below* the straight-line carrying amount). Scenario 3 in this playground walks through a concrete example.

- Challenge an auditor's computation
