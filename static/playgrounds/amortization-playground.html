2 The Six Methods
CA
Constant Annuity
Annuité Constante

Fixed total payment every period. Interest portion decreases while principal portion increases over time. The most common method for mortgages and consumer loans.

Formula
PMT = P × r(1+r)n / [(1+r)n − 1]
Algebraic
A = P · r(1+r)ⁿ / [(1+r)ⁿ − 1]
Payment stays constant throughout Early payments are mostly interest Most common for mortgages
CP
Constant Principal
Amortissement Constant

Fixed principal repayment each period. Total payment decreases over time as interest declines. Preferred for corporate and project finance.

Formula
Principal/period = Loan ÷ Number of periods
Algebraic
M = P/n, A₀ = M + B₀₋₁ · r
First payment is highest Total interest is lower than constant annuity Common in corporate lending
BU
Bullet / In-Fine
Remboursement In-Fine

Interest-only payments during the entire term. Full principal repaid in a single balloon payment at maturity. Used for short-term corporate debt and bonds.

Formula
Each period: Interest = P × r  |  Final: P + interest
Algebraic
I₀ = P · r,   Aₙ = P + P·r
No principal reduction until maturity Lowest periodic cost but highest total interest Common for bonds and bridge loans
PA
Progressive Annuity
Annuité Progressive

Payments increase by a fixed growth rate each period. Designed for borrowers who expect rising income. First payment is lower than constant annuity.

Formula
PMT₁ = P × (r−g) / [1−((1+g)/(1+r))n]
Algebraic
A₁ = P(r−g)/[1−((1+g)/(1+r))ⁿ],   A₀ = A₁(1+g)^(t−1)
Requires a growth rate input Lower initial burden than constant annuity Ideal for career-start borrowers
DP
Différé Partiel
Partial Grace Period

Interest-only payments during the grace period, then normal repayment begins. The principal remains unchanged during grace. Eases initial cash flow burden.

Formula
Grace: Payment = P × r  |  After: standard method on P
Algebraic
Grace: A₀ = P·r  |  Post: recalculate on remaining term
Principal stays unchanged during grace Total interest is higher than no-grace equivalent Common for student and startup loans
DT
Différé Total
Full Grace Period

No payments at all during the grace period. Interest accumulates and is added to the principal (capitalization). Repayment starts on the inflated principal.

Formula
Grace: New P = P × (1+r)grace  |  After: method on New P
Algebraic
P' = P(1+r)ᵍ, then standard amortization on P'
Interest capitalizes — principal grows! Most expensive option overall Used when borrower has zero initial cash flow
3 Formula Gallery
PMT (Annuity Payment)
PMT = P × r(1+r)n / [(1+r)n − 1]
P = principal   r = periodic rate   n = periods
Interest per Period
Interest = Opening Balance × Periodic Rate
Applies to every method — interest is always balance × rate
Term from Payment
n = −log(1 − P×r/PMT) / log(1+r)
Solve for number of periods given a known payment amount
Effective Rate (APR / TEG)
P = Σ [CF₀ / (1 + reff)t]
Solved iteratively — reff is the rate that makes NPV of all cash flows equal to loan amount
4 Key Concepts
Amortization vs Annuity
Amortization (Amortissement) = the principal repayment portion — always shown in INDIGO
Interest (Intérêts) = the cost of borrowing portion — always shown in AMBER
Payment (Échéance) = Amortization + Interest (+ Insurance if applicable)
Nominal Rate vs Effective Rate (TEG/APR)
Nominal rate: the advertised annual rate, divided by periods for each calculation
Effective rate (TEG/APR): the true annual cost including compounding and fees
TEG = Taux Effectif Global (French/OHADA term for APR)
TEG = (1 + rperiodic)n − 1
