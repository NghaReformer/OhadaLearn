**Explanation:** `src/lib/theme/css-generator.ts` emits `--green`, `--amber`, `--error`, `--accent` etc., but never `--orange`, `--blue`, or `--red`. Every `var(--orange, #f59e0b)` / `var(--blue, #3b82f6)` / `var(--red, #ef4444)` expression silently falls back to a hardcoded hex on every render. The theme is effectively non-functional for these 27 declarations — changing tokens has zero effect.

**Suggested fix:** Add `--orange`, `--blue`, `--red` aliases to `css-generator.ts` pointing to the nearest semantic tokens (`--amber`, `--accent`, `--error`), or rename all usages to the canonical token names already defined.

### Idea 1 — SYSCOHADA Balance Sheet Builder

**EN:** SYSCOHADA Balance Sheet Builder  
**FR:** Constructeur de Bilan SYSCOHADA

**Target learner:** Licence 2 / Licence 3

**Pedagogical objective:** Most students can post journal entries but cannot structure a SYSCOHADA bilan — they confuse the three-bloc layout (Actif immobilisé / Actif circulant / Trésorerie vs Capitaux propres / Dettes financières / Passif circulant / Trésorerie passif) with a simple two-column ledger. This playground fixes the structural mental model.

**Core interaction:** Student receives a randomized trial balance (pre-adjusted) and classifies each account into the correct SYSCOHADA bilan section by drag-and-drop or account-picker. The bilan renders live as they assign accounts. Balancing check runs on submit.

**Minimum viable inputs/outputs:**
- Input: trial balance with 15–25 accounts drawn from `ohada-accounts.ts`
- Output: rendered bilan with totals per section, balance-check badge, and per-line feedback on misclassifications

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** Journal Entry teaches *how* to record; this teaches *where the result lives*. It is the natural next step after any journal-entry exercise and directly uses `$lib/shared/chart-of-accounts` for account data. Implements `PlaygroundModule` with `category: 'financial-accounting'`, `standards: ['syscohada']`.

### Idea 2 — Cash Flow Statement (Indirect Method)

**EN:** Cash Flow Statement — Indirect Method  
**FR:** Tableau des Flux de Trésorerie — Méthode Indirecte

**Target learner:** Licence 3 / Master 1

**Pedagogical objective:** Students confuse profit with cash — the most persistent misconception in financial accounting. The indirect method reconciliation (net income → operating cash flow via non-cash adjustments) is heavily tested in DSCG/CCA exams but rarely practiced interactively.

**Core interaction:** Student starts from a given net income figure and a list of balance sheet movements (ΔReceivables, ΔInventory, Depreciation, ΔPayables, capital expenditures, loan proceeds). They classify each item as add-back, deduction, investing, or financing, and the three-section cash flow statement populates in real time.

**Minimum viable inputs/outputs:**
- Input: net income slider, 8–12 balance sheet deltas (numeric fields)
- Output: live three-section tableau (exploitation / investissement / financement), net cash change, reconciliation to opening/closing cash

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** TVM and Interest cover time-value mechanics; this covers cash-basis interpretation. Reuses `$lib/format/currency.ts` and the KpiStrip component for section totals.

### Idea 3 — Budget Variance Analysis

**EN:** Budget Variance Analyzer  
**FR:** Analyseur d'Écarts Budgétaires

**Target learner:** Master 1 / practicing professional

**Pedagogical objective:** Budget variance decomposition (price variance, quantity/efficiency variance, mixed variance) is a core managerial accounting skill that students mechanically memorize without understanding which variance managers can actually control. The interactive format forces them to see why the split matters.

**Core interaction:** Student enters actual and budgeted unit price, actual and budgeted volume, and actual and budgeted fixed costs for a scenario. The playground decomposes the total variance into price, quantity, and mixed components on a waterfall chart, with color coding (favorable = green, unfavorable = red/amber).

**Minimum viable inputs/outputs:**
- Inputs: budgeted price, actual price, budgeted volume, actual volume, budgeted fixed cost, actual fixed cost
- Outputs: total variance, price variance, quantity variance, mixed variance, waterfall chart, management interpretation callout

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** CVP shows the break-even structure; this shows the *deviation analysis* once operations are underway. It is the bridge between planning (CVP) and control (variance reporting). Implements `category: 'managerial-accounting'`.

### Idea 5 — Provisions & Impairment Simulator

**EN:** Provisions & Impairment Simulator  
**FR:** Simulateur de Dépréciations et Provisions

**Target learner:** Licence 3 / Master 1

**Pedagogical objective:** Students conflate *dépréciations d'actifs* (asset-specific write-downs, e.g. créances douteuses) with *provisions pour risques et charges* (liability-side estimates for future obligations). SYSCOHADA treats them in different account classes (29x vs 15x) and students routinely debit the wrong account.

**Core interaction:** Student is presented with a scenario (e.g. a doubtful receivable, a pending lawsuit, an inventory write-down). They must choose the correct provision type, enter the estimated amount and probability, and construct the year-end journal entry. On submission, the simulator validates the debit/credit accounts against SYSCOHADA classes, shows the balance sheet impact, and explains why the other provision type would have been wrong.

**Minimum viable inputs/outputs:**
- Inputs: scenario selector (5 scenario types), provision amount, probability/percentage slider
- Outputs: validated journal entry, balance sheet extract showing asset net value or liability increase, SYSCOHADA account class explanation panel

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** Depreciation (static HTML) covers scheduled asset amortization; this covers *judgment-based* write-downs and contingency provisions — a fundamentally different accounting decision. Uses `PlaygroundModule` with `standards: ['syscohada']`, `category: 'financial-accounting'`.
