### BUG-02 — Fully hardcoded color map in `CategoryBreakdownDonut.svelte`
**Severity:** Medium
**File:** `src/lib/playgrounds/bank-reconciliation/components/CategoryBreakdownDonut.svelte:31-39`

**Explanation:** A JS-side `const CATEGORY_COLORS` map assigns raw hex strings (9 entries: `#6ea8fe`, `#22c55e`, `#f59e0b`, `#10b981`, `#ef4444`, `#a855f7`, `#06b6d4`, `#ec4899`, `#fb923c`) directly to SVG fill attributes, with no `var()` wrapper at all. These colors are completely invisible to the theme system and cannot be changed via `tokens.ts`. ### BUG-03 — `var(--green, #16a34a)` fallback disagrees with the defined token
**Severity:** Low
**File:** `src/lib/playgrounds/bank-reconciliation/components/ReconciliationStatement.svelte:275,292,293`

**Explanation:** `--green` is defined and emitted as `#34d399` (mint, high-luminance). The fallback `#16a34a` is a dark forest green — roughly 4× darker. **Suggested fix:** Remove the fallback: `color: var(--green);` — consistent with `PlaygroundCard.svelte:58` and `ShareButton.svelte:83-84`.

### BUG-05 — `a11y_click_events_have_key_events` suppressed on backdrop without semantic role
**Severity:** Low
**File:** `src/lib/components/feedback/FeedbackDrawer.svelte:71-78`

**Explanation:** The backdrop `<div>` suppresses two Svelte a11y warnings and carries `role="presentation"`. While `onkeydown` is present and handles Escape, `role="presentation"` strips all semantic meaning and discards any AT awareness of the element. Screen-reader users who navigate outside the drawer with arrow keys get no announcement of the dimmed content. The `role="presentation"` approach is valid for purely decorative elements, but a click-dismissible overlay carries interaction intent.

**Suggested fix:** Replace `role="presentation"` with `role="none"` (identical semantics, but explicit) and add `tabindex="-1"` to prevent the backdrop itself from receiving focus, or restructure the overlay as a `<button class="backdrop" aria-label="Close feedback">` to eliminate the a11y suppression entirely.

### BUG-06 — `state_referenced_locally` suppressed in `PlaygroundShell` and `GoalSeekPopover`
**Severity:** Low
**Files:**
- `src/lib/components/playground/PlaygroundShell.svelte:51`
- `src/lib/playgrounds/interest/components/GoalSeekPopover.svelte:43,45`

**Explanation:** The `state_referenced_locally` warning fires when a `$state` variable is read in the same reactive scope it was declared, preventing Svelte from tracking changes through the reactive graph. Suppressing this warning rather than refactoring the state out of the closure hides a potential stale-read problem. If the popover or shell state gets updated while the local snapshot is live, the UI may not re-render.

**Suggested fix:** Audit the suppressed code paths to determine if the state should be derived (`$derived`) or lifted to a parent component. If the snapshot-on-open pattern is intentional (e.g. capturing initial value), extract it into a named variable initialized from `$state` at mount time rather than inside a reactive closure.

### PG-01 — VAT / TVA Journal Reconciliation
**Title EN:** VAT Cycle & Journal Entries
**Title FR:** Cycle TVA et Écritures Comptables

**Target learner:** Licence 2 / Licence 3

**Pedagogical objective:** Students systematically confuse TVA collectée (output VAT), TVA déductible (input VAT), and TVA à payer — especially the netting mechanism and the journal entry treatment under SYSCOHADA accounts 4431, 4432, 4456, 4457. No existing playground covers tax accounting.

**Core interaction:** Learner enters a set of purchase and sale invoices (HT amount + TVA rate). The playground computes TVA collectée, TVA déductible, and net TVA due. Learner then constructs the month-end netting journal entry from scratch using the AccountPicker component; the grader checks debit/credit assignment against SYSCOHADA class 4 accounts.

**Minimum viable inputs / outputs:**
- Inputs: 3–6 invoice lines (purchase/sale toggle, HT amount, rate selector: 0%/5%/10%/18%/19.25%)
- Outputs: TVA summary strip (collectée, déductible, solde net), journal entry workspace, pass/fail grading with highlighted errors

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** Extends `journal-entry` into a tax-specific scenario; reuses `AccountPicker`, the grading engine from `src/lib/grading/journal-entry.ts`, and the existing `NumberField` component. Closes a major SYSCOHADA curriculum gap — TVA is tested on every DSCG exam.

### PG-02 — Financial Ratio Dashboard
**Title EN:** Financial Ratio Analysis (SYSCOHADA)
**Title FR:** Analyse des Ratios Financiers (SYSCOHADA)

**Target learner:** Licence 3 / Master 1

**Pedagogical objective:** Students can compute individual ratios but rarely understand how ratios interact, what "healthy" ranges look like for OHADA-zone firms, or how a single balance-sheet change cascades across solvency, liquidity, and profitability ratios simultaneously. Existing playgrounds compute single financial quantities; none visualize a complete ratio dashboard.

**Core interaction:** Learner edits a simplified SYSCOHADA balance sheet and income statement (actif, passif, compte de résultat — 8–12 line items). The playground computes 12 key ratios in real time (liquidité générale, solvabilité, endettement, rentabilité économique, BFR, fond de roulement, etc.) and displays them as a radar chart with OHADA-zone benchmarks shown as a reference polygon. A "stress test" slider lets learners inflate debt or shrink revenue and watch which ratios breach thresholds first.

**Minimum viable inputs / outputs:**
- Inputs: Grouped numeric fields for actif immobilisé, actif circulant, capitaux propres, dettes financières, CA, résultat net
- Outputs: Ratio table with status indicators (green/amber/red vs benchmark), radar chart, top-3 alert panel

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** `cvp` covers operating leverage; this covers the balance-sheet side. Reuses `fmtNumber`, `fmtPct` from `src/lib/format`, the KPI strip pattern from `KpiStrip.svelte`, and the chart infrastructure from the CVP playground. Directly addresses the "Analyse financière" module present in every OHADA-zone Licence 3 programme.

Fills a gap that affects every company that operates in the OHADA zone.

### PG-04 — Foreign Currency Transactions & Translation
**Title EN:** Foreign Currency Accounting (SYSCOHADA)
**Title FR:** Comptabilité en Devises (SYSCOHADA)

**Target learner:** Licence 3 / Master / practicing professional

**Pedagogical objective:** SYSCOHADA OHADA Acte Uniforme requires monetary items to be retranslated at closing rates with differences recorded in account 476/477 (écarts de conversion actif/passif) — a rule students routinely misapply by using the transaction rate instead. No existing playground addresses foreign currency.

**Core interaction:** Learner enters a foreign-currency receivable or payable (amount, currency, transaction date rate, and year-end rate). The playground computes the initial recognition entry, the year-end revaluation, and the settlement entry — three sequential journal-entry workspaces graded against SYSCOHADA rules. A currency timeline chart shows the rate movement and highlights the exchange-gain/-loss windows.

**Minimum viable inputs / outputs:**
- Inputs: Transaction currency (USD/EUR/GBP from the existing 16-currency list), transaction amount, transaction rate, closing rate, settlement rate
- Outputs: Three graded journal-entry panels (initial, revaluation, settlement), P&L impact summary, currency-rate timeline

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** `interest` and `tvm` cover time-value effects on same-currency flows; this covers cross-currency accounting. Reuses `format/currency.ts` (already supports USD, EUR, GBP alongside XOF/XAF), `AccountPicker`, the journal-entry grading engine, and the `finance/` utility layer. Fills a high-difficulty exam topic with zero current coverage.

### PG-05 — Working Capital & Cash-Flow Cycle
**Title EN:** Working Capital & Operating Cycle
**Title FR:** Besoin en Fonds de Roulement et Cycle d'Exploitation

**Target learner:** Licence 3 / Master 1

**Pedagogical objective:** Students know the BFR formula but cannot reason about how changes to credit terms, inventory turnover, or supplier payment delays change cash flow timing — the operational intuition behind the formula is missing. Existing playgrounds (CVP, TVM) are static-period tools; none simulate a multi-period operating cycle.

**Core interaction:** Learner sets three dials: "délai client" (0–90 days), "délai fournisseur" (0–90 days), and "durée de stockage" (0–60 days). The playground animates an operating cycle timeline showing when cash leaves (supplier payment), when inventory converts to receivables (sale), and when cash returns (collection). A live BFR gauge updates in real time. A "what-if" table shows how a 15-day reduction in each lever changes BFR and the minimum cash reserve needed.

**Minimum viable inputs / outputs:**
- Inputs: CA annuel, achats, délai client, délai fournisseur, durée de stockage
- Outputs: BFR amount, operating cycle animation, what-if table (3 rows × 3 levers), funding gap vs cash position

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** `amortization` and `tvm` handle investment financing; this handles operational financing. Pure SVG animation approach (no external chart library needed), reusing the existing animation token (`--transition-spring`) and the `KpiStrip` component for key metrics. Addresses the most-tested topic in "Finance d'entreprise" across OHADA-zone Licence programmes.
