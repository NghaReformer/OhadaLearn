### MEDIUM — A11Y: `<svg role="img">` without an accessible name

**Files:**
- `src/lib/playgrounds/interest/components/SimpleGrowthChart.svelte:105`
- `src/lib/playgrounds/interest/components/CompoundGrowthChart.svelte:98`

Both charts declare `role="img"` on the `<svg>` element but provide no accessible name: no `aria-label`, no `aria-labelledby`, and no `<title>` child element. A `<figcaption>` sits outside the SVG and is not associated. Screen readers will announce an unlabeled image.

**Suggested fix:** Add a `<title>` as the first child of each `<svg>`, keyed through i18n, and add `aria-labelledby` referencing a unique ID:

```svelte
<svg role="img" aria-labelledby="simple-chart-title" ...>
  <title id="simple-chart-title">{$t('int.chart.simple.title')}</title>
  ...
</svg>
```

The ChartPanel in the amortization playground (`src/lib/playgrounds/amortization/components/ChartPanel.svelte:239–311`) uses `role="figure"` + `aria-label` on a wrapper `<div>` — that pattern is correct and should be replicated here.

### Idea 1 — TVA Calculator / Calculateur de TVA

**Title (EN):** Value-Added Tax Calculator  
**Title (FR):** Calculateur de TVA  
**Target learner:** Licence 2, practicing professional

**Pedagogical objective:** Many students confuse HT (hors taxes), TVA collectée, TVA déductible, and TVA à décaisser. This playground fixes the misconception that TVA is a cost to the enterprise rather than a pass-through.

**Core interaction:** The learner enters purchase invoices (HT + taux) and sales invoices (HT + taux), then manually computes TVA collectée, TVA déductible, and the net amount owed to or recoverable from the tax authority. The playground validates each step.

**Minimum viable inputs/outputs:**
- Inputs: N purchase lines and N sale lines (HT amount, TVA rate from OHADA-zone rate table: 0%, 10%, 18%, 19.25%)
- Outputs: TVA collectée, TVA déductible, solde TVA (à payer / crédit d'impôt), journal entry preview

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** Journal Entry already handles the debit/credit logic; TVA Calculator teaches the economic calculation upstream of the journal entry. Shared `NumberField` + `$format` system applies directly. Fits `PlaygroundCategory = 'financial-accounting'`.

### Idea 2 — Financial Ratio Analyzer / Analyseur de Ratios Financiers

**Title (EN):** Financial Ratio Analyzer  
**Title (FR):** Analyseur de Ratios Financiers  
**Target learner:** Licence 3, Master

**Pedagogical objective:** Students often memorize ratio formulas without understanding what balance sheet line-items to use under SYSCOHADA (which uses different French-language labels than IFRS). This playground grounds the formulas in real SYSCOHADA balance sheet structure.

**Core interaction:** Learner enters simplified SYSCOHADA balance sheet and income statement values (actif immobilisé, actif circulant, dettes, résultat, chiffre d'affaires…). The playground computes a dashboard of 8–10 key ratios (liquidité générale, solvabilité, rentabilité économique, rotation des créances…) with color-coded benchmarks and explanations.

**Minimum viable inputs/outputs:**
- Inputs: ~15 balance sheet fields (SYSCOHADA structure), 3 income statement fields
- Outputs: Ratio dashboard with computed values, benchmark ranges, and one-sentence interpretation per ratio

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** TVM and CVP cover prospective cash-flow reasoning; this playground covers retrospective financial diagnosis. Shares the `format/` currency and percentage formatters. First playground to require multi-section structured input — a good test of `ExerciseInputSchema`.

### Idea 3 — Payroll Calculator / Calculateur de Paie

**Title (EN):** Payroll Calculator  
**Title (FR):** Calculateur de Paie  
**Target learner:** Licence 3, Master, practicing professional

**Pedagogical objective:** Payroll computation under OHADA-zone labour law (CNPS contributions, IRPP withholding, transport allowance, family allowances) is procedural and error-prone. This playground automates the net-pay decomposition so learners verify each line rather than memorizing a formula.

**Core interaction:** Learner enters gross salary (salaire brut), employment category, number of dependants, and country (Cameroun, Côte d'Ivoire, Sénégal — each has different CNPS/IRPP tables). The playground steps through: brut → cotisations salariales → brut imposable → IRPP → net à payer, with a salary slip layout and a matching journal entry for the employer's payroll charge.

**Minimum viable inputs/outputs:**
- Inputs: Salaire brut, pays, catégorie professionnelle, nombre de parts fiscales
- Outputs: Bulletin de paie interactif (step-by-step), journal entry for charges patronales

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** Bank Reconciliation handles cash; this playground handles the payroll obligation that generates the cash outflow. Uses `SharedResourceType = 'tax-tables'` already in the contract — the only playground so far that would exercise that resource type.

### Idea 4 — Accounts Receivable Aging / Balance Âgée Clients

**Title (EN):** Receivables Aging & DSO  
**Title (FR):** Balance Âgée Clients et DSO  
**Target learner:** Licence 3, Master, controller

**Pedagogical objective:** Students struggle to connect overdue invoice management with the need for provision for doubtful accounts (provisions pour créances douteuses) required under SYSCOHADA. The playground makes the aging-to-provision link explicit.

**Core interaction:** Learner enters a list of client invoices (date, amount, payment terms). The playground auto-ages them into buckets (current, 30, 60, 90, >90 days) and asks the learner to apply a provision rate per bucket, then compute the total provision and write the SYSCOHADA journal entry (debit Dotation aux provisions / credit Provision pour dépréciation).

**Minimum viable inputs/outputs:**
- Inputs: Up to 10 invoice lines (client name, amount, invoice date, payment terms)
- Outputs: Aging matrix, provision computation table, journal entry for the period-end provision

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** Bank Reconciliation handles cash receipts; this playground handles the credit-side of the cash cycle. Reuses the Journal Entry component's T-account display for the provision entry.

### Idea 5 — Cash Flow Statement Builder / Constructeur du Tableau des Flux

**Title (EN):** Cash Flow Statement Builder  
**Title (FR):** Constructeur du Tableau des Flux de Trésorerie  
**Target learner:** Master, practicing professional

**Pedagogical objective:** Constructing a SYSCOHADA-format Tableau des Flux de Trésorerie (TFT) via the indirect method is the hardest financial-statement exercise at Master level. The common error is double-counting changes in working capital or misclassifying financing vs. operating flows.

**Core interaction:** Learner is given a simplified SYSCOHADA balance sheet (two periods) and an income statement. They drag-and-drop each line item into the correct TFT section (operating / investing / financing) and enter the computed adjustment. The playground validates placement and arithmetic at each step, revealing how net income reconciles to net cash.

**Minimum viable inputs/outputs:**
- Inputs: 2-year comparative balance sheet (~20 lines), income statement (résultat net, amortissements, variation BFR)
- Outputs: Completed 3-section TFT with running totals, validation feedback per line, final reconciliation to opening/closing cash

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** This is the most advanced proposal and the only playground that synthesises all prior concepts (journal entries → trial balance → financial statements → cash flows). The drag-and-drop classification interaction is novel in the playground system and would require extending `ExerciseInputSchema` with a `classification` input type.
