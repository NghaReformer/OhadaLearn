## 2. New Playground Ideas

Existing playgrounds: **Journal Entry**, **Time Value of Money (TVM)**, **Cost-Volume-Profit (CVP)**, **Amortization / Depreciation**. None of the ideas below duplicate these.

---

### P1 — Partnership Profit Distribution

**EN:** Partnership Profit Distribution · **FR:** Répartition du Résultat en Société de Personnes

**Target learner:** Licence 2/3, practicing professionals

**Pedagogical objective:** Students consistently confuse the three-step SYSCOHADA profit-sharing sequence (interest on capital → management salaries → residual split by ratio) and make sign errors when a net loss must be absorbed. This playground forces them to build each step explicitly.

**Core interaction:** Learner inputs partner names, capital contributions, agreed salary allowances, and residual sharing percentages. The playground steps through each allocation tier, showing running balances and flagging when residual profit goes negative (loss absorption). Final output is the journal entries to close the profit account to each partner's current account.

**Minimum viable inputs / outputs:**
- Inputs: net income, number of partners (2–5), capital per partner, salary allowance per partner, residual % per partner, interest rate on capital
- Outputs: allocation table by tier, partner current-account movements, closing journal entries (linking to the journal-entry playground's account system)

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** The journal-entry playground teaches mechanics; this one teaches the higher-level decision of *what* to record at year-end. It naturally sequences after amortization (the profit figure is known only after depreciation charges).

---

### P2 — VAT / TVA Reconciliation Simulator

**EN:** VAT Period Reconciliation · **FR:** Rapprochement et Déclaration de la TVA

**Target learner:** Licence 2/3

**Pedagogical objective:** Many students can post a single VAT-inclusive invoice but cannot reconcile a full month of input/output VAT to determine the amount payable or the credit to carry forward — a core tax compliance skill in OHADA-zone countries (Côte d'Ivoire, Cameroon, Senegal all use 18–19.25% VAT).

**Core interaction:** Learner builds a simplified VAT register: adds sales invoices (with or without exemptions) and purchase invoices (deductible vs. non-deductible). The playground accumulates output VAT and input VAT, shows the net position, and generates the declaration summary entry. A toggle lets the learner switch between the direct-deduction method and the payment method.

**Minimum viable inputs / outputs:**
- Inputs: invoices with HT amount, VAT rate (configurable: 18%, 19.25%), deductible flag
- Outputs: output VAT total, deductible input VAT total, TVA due or credit, month-end journal entry (compte 4441/4452 per SYSCOHADA class 4)

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** Extends the journal-entry playground into the tax domain; TVM and CVP are silent on indirect taxation.

---

### P3 — Financial Ratio Dashboard

**EN:** Financial Ratio Dashboard · **FR:** Tableau de Bord des Ratios Financiers

**Target learner:** Licence 3, Master

**Pedagogical objective:** Students memorize ratio formulas but cannot interpret them in context or understand how a single accounting decision (e.g. capitalising vs expensing) moves multiple ratios simultaneously. This playground makes the feedback loop instantaneous.

**Core interaction:** Learner is given a pre-populated balance sheet and income statement (with numbers that can be tweaked via sliders). The dashboard computes and visualises 12–15 ratios grouped into four families: liquidity, solvency, profitability, activity. Each ratio shows its formula, computed value, and a benchmark band (OHADA sector averages where available). Tweaking an input causes affected ratios to animate to their new values.

**Minimum viable inputs / outputs:**
- Inputs: balance sheet line items (fixed assets, inventories, receivables, cash, equity, long-term debt, current liabilities), revenue, EBITDA, net income
- Outputs: current ratio, quick ratio, debt/equity, interest coverage, ROE, ROA, asset turnover, inventory days, receivable days, gross margin, net margin; colour-coded against thresholds; brief explanatory text per ratio

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** This playground operates *downstream* of everything else — it consumes the financial statements that journal entries and amortization charges produce, closing the educational loop.

### P4 — Year-End Accruals & Deferrals Builder

**EN:** Year-End Accruals & Deferrals · **FR:** Régularisation des Charges et Produits (Inventaire)

**Target learner:** Licence 2

**Pedagogical objective:** The *opérations d'inventaire* (year-end adjustments) are the most error-prone area of the SYSCOHADA exam. Students struggle to determine whether a prepaid charge increases or decreases the current-year expense and which direction the adjustment entry goes.

**Core interaction:** Learner is presented with a list of scenarios (insurance premium paid in advance, rent received in advance, accrued wages, interest earned but not yet invoiced). For each scenario, they input the amounts and period boundaries; the playground computes the adjustment amount and the correct journal entry. A timeline visualisation shows the fiscal year boundary and shades the portion that belongs to the current vs. next period.

**Minimum viable inputs / outputs:**
- Inputs: transaction amount, transaction date, coverage start/end dates, account type (charge or produit), nature (prepaid, accrued)
- Outputs: amount to defer/accrue, journal entry (compte 4X per SYSCOHADA), trial-balance impact, income statement impact

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** Shares the journal-entry engine and account code vocabulary; amortization handles tangible assets while this covers period-boundary timing differences — the two together cover the full scope of year-end adjustments.

### P5 — Loan Amortization vs. IFRS 9 / SYSCOHADA Interest Calculator

**EN:** Effective Interest Rate vs. Nominal Rate · **FR:** Taux d'Intérêt Effectif vs. Taux Nominal (Méthode du TIE)

**Target learner:** Master, practicing professionals

**Pedagogical objective:** SYSCOHADA Revised (2017) and IFRS 9 both require financial instruments to be measured at amortised cost using the effective interest rate (EIR / TIE). Students who can build a straight-line amortisation schedule (already covered) typically cannot set up the EIR schedule or explain why carrying amounts differ between methods.

**Core interaction:** Learner enters a loan's face value, coupon rate, market rate at issuance, and term. The playground computes the issue price (present value), builds both a nominal and an EIR amortisation table side by side, and visualises the carrying-amount divergence over time. A toggle switches between SYSCOHADA presentation (tableau de remboursement) and IFRS 9 presentation.

**Minimum viable inputs / outputs:**
- Inputs: face value, coupon rate, EIR / market rate, term in years, payment frequency (annual/semi-annual), standard toggle (SYSCOHADA / IFRS 9)
- Outputs: issue price, period-by-period table (cash interest, EIR interest expense, discount/premium amortisation, carrying amount), graph of carrying value over time, total interest cost comparison

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** The existing amortization playground covers straight-line depreciation of tangible assets; this one covers financial instrument amortisation at EIR — a distinct IFRS/SYSCOHADA Revised topic that shares the TVM engine's discount-rate mechanics.
