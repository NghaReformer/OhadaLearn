### Playground 1 — Balance Sheet Builder / Constructeur de Bilan SYSCOHADA

**Target learner:** Licence 2–3

**Pedagogical objective:** Students routinely confuse trial balance with financial statements. This playground bridges the gap: given a populated trial balance, the learner must classify each account into the correct SYSCOHADA balance sheet class (Assets classes 2–5 vs. Liabilities/Equity classes 1, 4, 5) and watch the statement self-assemble with live totals. It corrects the misconception that "the numbers are already there — you just need to copy them."

**Core interaction:** A two-column drag-and-drop interface. Left column: shuffled list of general ledger accounts with balances. Right column: a skeleton SYSCOHADA bilan with collapsed sections (Immobilisations, Stocks, Créances, Trésorerie / Capitaux propres, Dettes financières, Dettes d'exploitation). The learner drags accounts into sections; the skeleton auto-totals; a `Actif = Passif` equality indicator turns green only when all placements are correct.

**Minimum viable inputs:** 10–15 pre-built account/balance pairs per scenario (3 scenarios: PME commerciale, société industrielle, ONG). **Outputs:** Completed bilan with section totals, equality check, and highlighted misclassifications.

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** The journal-entry playground covers debits and credits. This playground covers the next step — aggregation into a published statement — closing the loop from transaction to reporting.

### Playground 2 — Cash Flow Statement Builder / Tableau des Flux de Trésorerie (méthode indirecte)

**Target learner:** Licence 3 / Master 1

**Pedagogical objective:** The indirect method of the cash flow statement is consistently the most misunderstood statement in African accounting curricula. Students can produce a balance sheet and income statement but cannot reconcile net income to operating cash flow. This playground makes every adjustment step explicit.

**Core interaction:** Given pre-filled net income and a set of balance sheet change cards (increase in receivables, depreciation charge, increase in inventory, etc.), the learner chooses "add" or "subtract" for each adjustment. A running total of operating cash flow updates live. A second section handles investing and financing activities via simple sign toggles. Final output is a complete three-section cash flow statement.

**Minimum viable inputs:** Net income, 6–8 balance sheet change items, 2–3 investing items, 2–3 financing items. **Outputs:** Three-section statement with subtotals; net change in cash reconciled to opening/closing cash; error highlighting for wrong-direction adjustments.

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** Completes the trio of primary financial statements alongside the balance sheet builder above. Also reinforces TVM intuition (cash timing matters) from the existing TVM playground.

### Playground 3 — Financial Ratios Dashboard / Tableau de Bord des Ratios Financiers

**Target learner:** Licence 3 / Master / practicing professional

**Pedagogical objective:** Ratio analysis is taught as a list of formulas but rarely as an integrated diagnostic tool. Students fail to interpret ratios in context — a current ratio of 1.2 means very different things for a supermarket vs. a construction company. This playground teaches ratio interpretation, not just computation.

**Core interaction:** Two-panel layout. Left: a simplified balance sheet + income statement with sliders for key line items (total current assets, current liabilities, net income, equity, total debt). Right: a live dashboard of 8–10 ratios grouped by category (liquidity, solvency, profitability, activity) with sparkline trend indicators and color-coded benchmarks. Each ratio card has an expandable "what this means" panel in EN/FR.

**Minimum viable inputs:** ~12 financial statement line item sliders with realistic OHADA-zone defaults. **Outputs:** Live ratio values, benchmark comparison bands (industry-average ranges sourced from SYSCOHADA pedagogy), color-coded health signals.

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** Pure analysis layer that consumes the outputs that the balance sheet and journal-entry playgrounds produce. Also leverages the existing `format/` utilities (`fmtNumber`, `fmtPct`) heavily.

### Playground 4 — Variance Analysis / Analyse des Écarts Budgétaires

**Target learner:** Master 1–2 / practicing professional (management accounting focus)

**Pedagogical objective:** Budget variance analysis is a core management accounting skill tested in SYSCOHADA professional exams, but students struggle to decompose a total variance into price and volume/efficiency components. The misconception is treating all variances as a single number rather than a structured explanation of performance.

**Core interaction:** Given a standard cost card (unit material cost, labor hours, overhead rate) and a flexible budget, the learner enters actual results (units produced, actual material cost, actual hours). The playground decomposes the total variance live into: (1) price/rate variance, (2) usage/efficiency variance, and (3) volume variance — each with an arrow indicating favorable or unfavorable. A waterfall chart visualizes the variance bridge from budgeted to actual profit.

**Minimum viable inputs:** Standard vs. actual for materials (price + quantity), labor (rate + hours), fixed overhead. **Outputs:** Variance decomposition table, waterfall chart, favorable/unfavorable flags.

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** CVP covers breakeven; this playground covers what happens after you set the budget and performance diverges. Together they cover the full planning-to-control cycle in managerial accounting.

### Playground 5 — Leasing vs. Buying Calculator / Simulateur Location-Acquisition

**Target learner:** Licence 3 / Master / practicing professional

**Pedagogical objective:** The lease-or-buy decision is a real-world application that synthesizes TVM, depreciation, and financial statement impact — three topics already covered by separate playgrounds. Students are rarely shown how these interact in a single decision model. Under SYSCOHADA, finance leases (crédit-bail) have specific capitalization rules that differ from IFRS 16, making this directly curriculum-relevant.

**Core interaction:** Side-by-side comparison. Left panel: buy option (asset cost, depreciation method, loan terms). Right panel: lease option (monthly payment, lease term, residual value). Both panels show year-by-year cash flows and cumulative impact on total cost. A summary section shows the NPV breakeven interest rate at which buying and leasing are equivalent, and highlights the balance sheet impact under SYSCOHADA crédit-bail rules.

**Minimum viable inputs:** Asset value, useful life, loan interest rate, lease payment, discount rate. **Outputs:** Year-by-year cash flow comparison, NPV of each option, crossover rate, total cost of ownership, balance sheet delta.

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** Reuses the amortization engine conceptually (loan repayment schedule) and the depreciation logic (static playground). Frames TVM in a concrete decision context rather than an abstract mathematical exercise. Creates a natural progression: TVM → amortization → lease-vs-buy.
