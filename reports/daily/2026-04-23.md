### BUG-01 — Hardcoded `#fff` violates theme token contract

**Severity:** medium  
**Files:**

- `src/routes/[lang]/learn/+page.svelte:202`
- `src/routes/[lang]/chart-of-accounts/+page.svelte:423`
- `src/routes/[lang]/chart-of-accounts/+page.svelte:458`

CLAUDE.md mandates all colors via CSS custom properties. Four `color: #fff` declarations appear in `.btn-confirm`, `.cta-link`, `.framework-btn.active`, and `.class-chip.active` — all are text on accent or error-colored backgrounds.

**Suggested fix:** Replace each with `color: var(--text-primary)` (`#eef0f6`). The visual delta from `#fff` is negligible but the rule is strict and the token is correct for light-on-dark text.

### Playground A — Balance Sheet Builder
**FR:** Constructeur de Bilan Comptable

**Target learner:** Licence 1–2 (introductory financial accounting)

**Pedagogical objective:** Students routinely confuse the accounting equation (`Assets = Liabilities + Equity`) and make balance sheet entries that don't balance. This playground forces them to discover the constraint through direct manipulation rather than rote memorisation.

**Core interaction:** A drag-and-drop workspace with three columns — Assets, Liabilities, Equity. The learner places SYSCOHADA account classes (Class 1–5) into the correct column, enters balances, and a live balance indicator shows whether the sheet balances. A "Validate" step reveals misclassified items with explanatory feedback.

**Minimum viable inputs / outputs:**
- Input: 8–12 pre-seeded account rows (name, class, balance) — learner assigns column.
- Output: Running total per column, balance indicator (Assets − (Liabilities + Equity) = 0), colour-coded error rows, structured feedback on each mistake.

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** Journal Entry teaches individual debit/credit entries; Balance Sheet Builder shows their aggregate effect on the accounting equation — bridging transaction-level and statement-level thinking.

**Architecture note:** Implements `PlaygroundModule` from `src/lib/contracts/playground.ts`. Account data typed against existing SYSCOHADA class taxonomy. Drag events use native HTML5 drag-and-drop, no additional dependency.

### Playground B — VAT & Tax Cascade Calculator
**FR:** Simulateur de TVA et Cascade Fiscale

**Target learner:** Licence 3 / Master 1 (SYSCOHADA applied taxation)

**Pedagogical objective:** OHADA-zone countries apply VAT at different rates (18 % Côte d'Ivoire, 19.25 % Cameroon, etc.) and some have additional levies on top of VAT. Students systematically miscalculate the tax cascade and the recoverable portion for registered businesses. This playground makes the cascade transparent.

**Core interaction:** The learner selects a country (from an OHADA member list), enters a pre-tax purchase price, toggles whether the buyer is a VAT-registered entity, and the playground decomposes the final price into: pre-tax base, VAT amount, additional levies, recoverable credit, and net cost to buyer. A bar chart shows each component proportionally.

**Minimum viable inputs / outputs:**
- Inputs: Country selector (7 OHADA members with distinct rates), pre-tax amount, VAT-registered toggle, transaction type (goods / services).
- Outputs: Itemised tax breakdown table, recoverable vs. non-recoverable split, formatted using `src/lib/format/` currency formatters (16 currencies).

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** CVP already models cost structures; this playground handles the tax layer that practitioners must layer on top of cost analysis when pricing goods and services in the OHADA zone.

### Playground C — Financial Ratio Analyser
**FR:** Analyseur de Ratios Financiers

**Target learner:** Licence 3 / Master (financial analysis)

**Pedagogical objective:** Students can compute individual ratios but struggle to interpret them in context — is a current ratio of 1.4 strong or weak for this sector? This playground teaches ratio interpretation, not just calculation.

**Core interaction:** The learner is given a set of simplified financial statement line items (assets, liabilities, revenue, EBITDA) — either randomly generated or from a library of 5 OHADA-zone sector profiles (agriculture, services, manufacturing, trade, BTP). They compute ratios by filling in a formula (e.g. `Current Assets ÷ Current Liabilities`). The playground benchmarks the result against sector medians and a visual gauge shows whether the company is above or below median. An explanation panel reveals what extreme values signal.

**Minimum viable inputs / outputs:**
- Inputs: Company financials (pre-filled, editable); sector selector; 6 target ratios (liquidity, solvency, profitability, efficiency).
- Outputs: Computed ratio, sector median, percentile position, 2-sentence interpretation, corrective insight for out-of-range values.

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** TVM and Amortization focus on single-instrument calculations. Ratio Analyser shifts to whole-firm diagnostics and ties accounting numbers to business decision-making.

### Playground D — Break-Even Sensitivity Analysis
**FR:** Analyse de Sensibilité au Seuil de Rentabilité

**Target learner:** Licence 2–3 (management accounting)

**Pedagogical objective:** The existing CVP playground computes a single break-even point. In practice, learners need to understand how sensitive that point is to input changes. Students over-anchor on a single number and fail to think in ranges.

**Core interaction:** Two-panel layout. Left: the learner adjusts three sliders (fixed costs ±30 %, variable cost per unit ±30 %, selling price ±30 %). Right: a live break-even chart re-renders with a shaded "safe zone" band showing the range of break-even quantities across all combinations of the slider bounds. A tornado chart ranks which variable has the most impact.

**Minimum viable inputs / outputs:**
- Inputs: Base fixed cost, variable cost/unit, selling price; three sensitivity sliders.
- Outputs: Central break-even quantity, min/max range, tornado chart (3 bars), margin of safety at current volume.

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** This is explicitly designed as a follow-on to the CVP playground — students use CVP first to learn the concept, then use this to stress-test it. Keeps the complexity separated into two focused tools.

### Playground E — Payroll & Social Charge Simulator
**FR:** Simulateur de Paie et Charges Sociales

**Target learner:** Master / practicing professional (OHADA HR accounting)

**Pedagogical objective:** Payroll accounting is one of the most error-prone areas for junior accountants in the OHADA zone because social contribution rates differ by country and by employee category (cadre vs. non-cadre). The breakdown between employer cost, gross salary, employee deductions, and net pay is opaque.

**Core interaction:** The learner enters gross salary and selects country + employee category. The playground computes: employer social charges (CNPS, CRTV levy, etc. at country-specific rates), employee deductions, net taxable base, income tax slab computation (IRPP), and final net pay. A waterfall chart animates the deductions from gross to net. The learner can compare two employee profiles side by side.

**Minimum viable inputs / outputs:**
- Inputs: Gross salary, country (Cameroon, Côte d'Ivoire, Sénégal — highest-enrolment OHADA markets), employee category (cadre / non-cadre / expatriate), number of dependants.
- Outputs: Employer cost, gross salary, total employee deductions (itemised), net pay, effective tax rate, journal entries for the payroll charge (linking back to Journal Entry playground concepts).

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** No existing playground covers labour costs. This closes a major curriculum gap and is the most professionally relevant tool for students entering accounting practice in the OHADA zone.
