- **Severity:** high
- **File:** `src/routes/[lang]/privacy/+page.svelte:5–27`

### BUG-02 — Missing `--text-on-accent` token; `#fff` used raw on accent backgrounds
- **Severity:** medium
- **Files:**
  - `src/lib/playgrounds/journal-entry/components/EntryHistory.svelte:400`

- **Details:** All four instances are on `.active` states that set `background: var(--accent)`. The foreground is hardcoded `color: #fff`, bypassing the theme system. If the accent color is ever changed to a light value the text becomes invisible.

- **Details:** `tokens.ts` defines colors, radii, shadows, and transitions, but has no typography scale (heading sizes, body sizes, captions) and no spacing scale. As a result, dozens of components invent their own sizing values ad-hoc. This violates CLAUDE.md's rule that all spacing/fonts go through CSS custom properties from theme tokens.
- **Suggested fix:** Add `typography` and `spacing` maps to `ThemeTokens`, emit them in `css-generator.ts`, and replace literals incrementally. Even defining four or five named steps (`--text-xs`, `--text-sm`, `--text-base`, `--text-lg`, `--text-xl`) would eliminate most violations.

### IDEA-01 — Tableau des Soldes Intermédiaires de Gestion (SIG)
**Title EN:** Intermediate Management Balances  
**Title FR:** Tableau des Soldes Intermédiaires de Gestion  
**Target learner:** Licence 3 / Master 1  
**Pedagogical objective:** Students confuse net profit with operating performance. SIG forces them to decompose the income statement into nine successive margins (marge commerciale → valeur ajoutée → EBE → résultat d'exploitation → résultat net), making each layer's origin and meaning explicit.  
**Core interaction:** User enters revenue, purchases, external charges, payroll, depreciation, and financial charges. The playground recomputes each SIG line in real time, highlights which charges affect which margin, and annotates each line with its SYSCOHADA account class.  
**Minimum viable inputs:** Sales revenue, cost of goods sold, external services, wages & social charges, depreciation provision, financial charges, tax on profit.  
**Outputs:** Nine SIG rows with running balance, a waterfall chart, and a comparison pane contrasting SYSCOHADA SIG vs. French PCG SIG (where they diverge).  
**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** CVP covers the break-even decision; SIG covers post-period performance decomposition. Journal Entry lets students record — SIG lets them interpret.

### IDEA-02 — Évaluation des Stocks — FIFO / CUMP
**Title EN:** Inventory Valuation — FIFO & Weighted-Average Cost  
**Title FR:** Évaluation des Stocks — FIFO et CUMP  
**Target learner:** Licence 2  
**Pedagogical objective:** Students systematically mix up stock movement methods and get wrong closing inventory figures. The playground makes the mechanical difference tangible: the same transaction sequence produces a different balance sheet value under FIFO vs. CUMP.  
**Core interaction:** User builds a sequence of stock-in and stock-out movements (date, qty, unit cost). The playground renders two side-by-side stock cards — one computed under FIFO, one under CUMP (coût unitaire moyen pondéré) — and shows the resulting cost of goods sold and closing stock for each method.  
**Minimum viable inputs:** Up to 10 movement rows (type: entry/exit, quantity, unit cost); opening stock quantity and value.  
**Outputs:** Per-method stock card table, closing inventory value, COGS, and a highlighted difference panel quantifying the profit impact of the choice.  
**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** Bank Reconciliation covers the cash cycle; this covers the goods cycle. Depreciation (static HTML) covers fixed-asset cost allocation — this covers current-asset cost flow.

### IDEA-03 — Déclaration de TVA Mensuelle
**Title EN:** Monthly VAT Return  
**Title FR:** Déclaration de TVA Mensuelle  
**Target learner:** Licence 2 / practicing professional  
**Pedagogical objective:** Students understand VAT mechanically but cannot construct the monthly return. They struggle with identifying collectible TVA, deductible TVA, credit carry-forward, and the net payable, and often confuse invoice date with declaration period.  
**Core interaction:** User populates a mini-ledger of sales (HT amount, TVA rate) and purchases (HT amount, TVA rate, deductible: yes/no). The playground aggregates collectible and deductible TVA, applies the OHADA standard rates (18% / 0%), computes the net balance, and generates a simplified Form M declaration summary.  
**Minimum viable inputs:** Up to 8 sales rows and 8 purchase rows with HT amounts and rate selectors (0%, 18%); optional prior-month TVA credit.  
**Outputs:** Collectible TVA total, deductible TVA total, net TVA payable/credit, a "declaration summary" panel, and a toggle to see the matching journal entries.  
**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** Journal Entry teaches the debit/credit mechanic; this teaches the fiscal compliance workflow that relies on it. No existing playground addresses tax reporting.
