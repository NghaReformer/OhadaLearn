### Theme & Color Violations

#### B-01 — `--orange`, `--blue`, `--red` CSS variables never emitted — fallback hex fires everywhere
**Severity:** HIGH
**Files:**
- `src/lib/components/playground/KpiStrip.svelte:76`
- `src/lib/playgrounds/amortization/components/ChartPanel.svelte:436,444,448,493,501,515,529,533`
- `src/lib/playgrounds/amortization/components/ScheduleTable.svelte:240,246,259`
- `src/lib/playgrounds/amortization/components/InputsPanel.svelte:604,605`
- `src/lib/playgrounds/bank-reconciliation/components/LedgerPanel.svelte:175`

`css-generator.ts` emits `--amber`, `--green`, and `--error` but **not** `--orange`, `--blue`, or `--red`. Every rule using `var(--orange, #f59e0b)`, `var(--blue, #3b82f6)`, or `var(--red, #ef4444)` silently uses the hardcoded fallback hex — bypassing the theme system entirely.

**Fix:** Add `orange`, `orangeGlow`, `blue`, `blueGlow` to `ThemeTokens` and `defaultTheme` in `tokens.ts`; expose them in `css-generator.ts`. Map `--red` to the existing `--error` token instead of a new one.

#### B-02 — Raw `rgba()` tints in `ScheduleTable.svelte` bypass theme tokens

### P-01 — Inventory Valuation Methods (FIFO / CMUP)

**EN:** Inventory Valuation — FIFO vs. CMUP
**FR:** Valorisation des stocks — FIFO vs. CMUP

**Target learner:** Licence 2 / Licence 3

**Pedagogical objective:**
SYSCOHADA Révisé mandates either the First-In-First-Out (FIFO) or the Weighted-Average Moving-Unit-Cost (CMUP) method. Students routinely confuse the two: they forget that CMUP recalculates after every new purchase, while FIFO tracks discrete batches. The misconception causes systematic errors in COGS and in class-3 account balances.

**Core interaction:**
The learner builds a timeline of stock movements (initial balance, purchases, issues/sales) using a row-by-row form. Switching a toggle between FIFO and CMUP instantly recalculates the unit-cost column and closing inventory value — making the divergence visible in real time. A side-by-side diff view highlights rows where the two methods produce different unit costs.

**Minimum viable inputs / outputs:**
- Inputs: Item label, opening stock (qty + unit cost), list of purchases (date, qty, unit cost) and issues (date, qty).
- Outputs: Per-movement stock card with running unit cost and total value; COGS aggregate; closing inventory value; OHADA journal entries for each issue (Débit 601/607 / Crédit 311/371).

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:**
The Journal Entry playground teaches the entry structure; this playground shows exactly what figures feed into the debit side. Pairs with Amortization for end-of-period asset write-downs.

### P-02 — TVA / VAT Accounting (OHADA Zone)

**EN:** VAT Accounting — OHADA Zone
**FR:** Comptabilité TVA — zone OHADA

**Target learner:** Licence 2, practicing professionals

**Pedagogical objective:**
TVA rates vary across the OHADA zone (18 % in Côte d'Ivoire/Sénégal, 19.25 % in Cameroun, 20 % in Morocco, etc.). Students confuse TVA collectée (output VAT, account 4431) with TVA déductible (input VAT, accounts 4452/4455), and routinely compute the monthly payable incorrectly by mixing partial-deductibility rules.

**Core interaction:**
A country selector pre-loads the correct standard rate and any reduced rates. The learner enters invoices (purchase or sale, amount HT, rate, deductibility category). The tool auto-splits each line into HT / TVA / TTC and accumulates the declaration totals. A period-close panel computes either the TVA à payer or crédit to carry forward, with full journal entry output.

**Minimum viable inputs / outputs:**
- Inputs: Country (drives rate), invoice list (type, HT amount, TVA rate, deductibility %), period (month/year).
- Outputs: Declaration summary (collectée, déductible, net payable/credit), individual invoice breakdown, OHADA journal entries for each invoice and for the period-close declaration entry.

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:**
TVA entries appear in almost every Journal Entry scenario but are never explained mechanically. This bridges the procedural gap and reuses the AccountPicker component for 44xx accounts.

### P-03 — Financial Ratios & SYSCOHADA Analysis

**EN:** Financial Ratio Analyzer
**FR:** Analyseur de ratios financiers SYSCOHADA

**Target learner:** Licence 3, Master 1

**Pedagogical objective:**
Students calculate individual ratios in isolation during exams but cannot interpret a ratio profile holistically. The SYSCOHADA annual report (états financiers de synthèse) has a specific layout for the bilan and compte de résultat; ratio thresholds discussed in Francophone curricula differ from IFRS-based benchmarks.

**Core interaction:**
Two-panel entry form: simplified SYSCOHADA bilan (8 aggregate lines) and compte de résultat (6 aggregate lines). On change, 18 ratios update live — liquidity (générale, réduite, immédiate), solvency (gearing, debt coverage), profitability (ROE, ROA, EBITDA margin), and activity (inventory days, receivables days). Each ratio row shows the computed value, a benchmark range, and a one-line interpretation. A radar chart gives an at-a-glance profile.

**Minimum viable inputs / outputs:**
- Inputs: 14 numerical cells (bilan + P&L key lines).
- Outputs: Ratio table with 3-column display (value / benchmark / status), radar chart.

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:**
Amortization affects fixed assets and depreciation, directly changing the bilan inputs. The Journal Entry playground shows how entries close into the bilan; this playground lets students see the analytical consequence.

### P-04 — Payroll & Social Contributions (CNPS)

**EN:** Payroll Accounting — CNPS/CNAS
**FR:** Bulletin de paie et cotisations sociales

**Target learner:** Licence 3, practicing HR/admin professionals

**Pedagogical objective:**
CNPS contribution rates (Caisse Nationale de Prévoyance Sociale) differ by country and benefit category. Students can find the tables but cannot correctly split employer vs. employee shares, apply IRPP brackets (impôt sur le revenu des personnes physiques), or produce the resulting journal entry that books both the net salary (421) and the full employer social cost (431, 432, 447).

**Core interaction:**
Country selector pre-loads CNPS rates (Cameroun, Côte d'Ivoire, Sénégal, DRC presets). The learner enters gross salary, allowances (transport, housing), and number of dependents. The tool computes the pay slip in real time: each deduction line with its rate and amount, net salary, and employer total cost. A toggle shows the resulting OHADA journal entries (Débit 661/664 / Crédit 421/431/432/447).

**Minimum viable inputs / outputs:**
- Inputs: Country, gross salary, transport allowance, housing allowance, dependents count.
- Outputs: Pay slip (salarial deductions, net to pay), employer charges table, full journal entry.

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:**
Payroll charges are a recurring line in the CVP playground's fixed-cost inputs. Combining the two lets a student trace a salary cost from the pay-slip computation to the break-even impact.

### P-05 — Cash Budget (Tableau de Trésorerie Prévisionnel)

**EN:** Cash Budget / Treasury Forecast
**FR:** Tableau de trésorerie prévisionnelle

**Target learner:** Licence 3, Master 1, practicing financial managers

**Pedagogical objective:**
The tableau de trésorerie is a core SYSCOHADA management tool, but textbook exercises present static 12-month tables that students fill in without understanding the feedback loop: negative months trigger a credit-facility draw, which itself creates interest charges in subsequent months. Students conflate cash with profit and miss the timing differences.

**Core interaction:**
A 12-column grid (months) × N-row structure (receipt categories: client collections, asset disposals; disbursement categories: supplier payments, payroll, TVA, capex, loan repayments). Opening balance is editable. Each month's closing balance = opening + receipts – disbursements. Months with negative closing balance are highlighted in red with an optional "credit facility" toggle that automatically draws and repays, adding interest to the disbursements. A line chart shows the cash position over the year.

**Minimum viable inputs / outputs:**
- Inputs: Opening balance, 12-month amounts per row, credit facility limit and rate.
- Outputs: Monthly closing balances, cumulative flow line chart, negative-month count, total interest cost of the facility.

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:**
Loan repayment rows can be imported from the Amortization playground (share-state URL). Sales receipts are driven by the volume computed in the CVP break-even playground, closing a practical planning loop that no single existing playground covers.
