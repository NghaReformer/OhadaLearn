### MEDIUM

**M1 — `requestFullscreen()` / `exitFullscreen()` promises are unhandled**
`src/routes/[lang]/playgrounds/[slug]/+page.svelte:47–52`

```ts
target.requestFullscreen();   // returns Promise<void>, not awaited
isFullscreen = true;          // set eagerly before operation completes
```

If the browser rejects the fullscreen request (e.g., the user denies the permission prompt or the document is cross-origin), the rejected promise is unhandled and `isFullscreen` remains incorrectly `true`. A `fullscreenchange` event will never fire to correct it.

**Suggested fix:**
```ts
async function toggleFullscreen() {
  const target = data.isNativeModule ? nativeContainerEl : iframeEl;
  if (!target) return;
  try {
    if (!document.fullscreenElement) await target.requestFullscreen();
    else await document.exitFullscreen();
  } catch { /* user denied / browser blocked — state corrected by fullscreenchange listener */ }
}
```
Remove the manual `isFullscreen = true/false` assignments; let the existing `fullscreenchange` listener own the state.

### P1 — Statement of Cash Flows Builder
**EN:** Cash Flow Statement Builder
**FR:** Constructeur du Tableau des Flux de Trésorerie (TFT)

**Target learner:** Licence 3, Master 1

**Pedagogical objective:** Students consistently misclassify cash flows by activity (operating vs. investing vs. financing) and confuse the indirect method reconciliation adjustments (add back depreciation, adjust for working capital changes) with actual cash movements. This playground builds the TFT step-by-step from IS and BS data.

**Core interaction:** The learner is given a simplified income statement and comparative balance sheet. They drag line items into three activity buckets and confirm whether each is a source or use of cash. In indirect-method mode they adjust net income for non-cash items and working capital changes and watch the operating cash subtotal update in real-time.

**Minimum viable inputs/outputs:**
- Inputs: Net income, depreciation/amortisation, changes in trade receivables, inventory, payables, CAPEX, loan proceeds/repayments, dividends
- Outputs: Formatted SYSCOHADA TFT with subtotals per activity, net change in cash, closing cash balance; validation feedback on misclassified items

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** Journal Entry covers recognition-stage entries; this playground covers the cash-basis extraction step that confuses students after they master accrual accounting. Together they show the full information cycle from transaction → IS/BS → cash flow.

### P2 — Working Capital & Liquidity Analysis
**EN:** Working Capital & Liquidity Ratios
**FR:** Fonds de Roulement, BFR & Ratios de Liquidité

**Target learner:** Licence 2, Licence 3

**Pedagogical objective:** Students confuse the three SYSCOHADA liquidity indicators: Fonds de Roulement (FR), Besoin en Fonds de Roulement (BFR), and Trésorerie Nette (TN = FR − BFR). They compute each in isolation without understanding the structural relationship between them or what a negative BFR means for a retail business versus a manufacturing firm.

**Core interaction:** The learner inputs a simplified SYSCOHADA balance sheet (actif immobilisé, actif circulant, dettes financières, dettes d'exploitation). Three live gauges show FR, BFR, and TN. Sliders let them manipulate individual balance sheet items and observe how the liquidity position changes. A scenario switcher presents industry archetypes (supermarket with negative BFR vs. manufacturer with positive BFR).

**Minimum viable inputs/outputs:**
- Inputs: Actif stable, passif stable, actif circulant d'exploitation, passif circulant d'exploitation
- Outputs: FR / BFR / TN with sign interpretation, quick-ratio and current-ratio, colour-coded commentary (healthy / tight / critical)

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** Bank Reconciliation addresses short-term cash matching at the bank level; this playground addresses the structural working-capital question that drives whether a company needs a credit line. The two together cover the full treasury management picture required for SYSCOHADA licence programmes.

### P3 — VAT Journal Entry Drill (OHADA Zone Rates)
**EN:** VAT Accounting Drill
**FR:** Exerciseur de Comptabilisation de la TVA

**Target learner:** Licence 2, working students in the OHADA zone

**Pedagogical objective:** Miscoding TVA entries is the single most common error in SYSCOHADA accounting exams. Students confuse comptes 4452 (TVA récupérable sur achats), 4455 (TVA collectée sur ventes), 4456 (TVA à décaisser), and 4457. This playground builds muscle memory through repetition on realistic invoice scenarios.

**Core interaction:** The learner sees an invoice description (e.g., "Purchased office equipment, 2 400 000 XAF HT, TVA 18%"). They must construct the full journal entry including TVA accounts and verify that TVA récupérable minus TVA collectée yields the correct settlement amount. Exercises progress from simple purchase/sale to mixed scenarios with TVA non-déductible on passenger vehicles.

**Minimum viable inputs/outputs:**
- Inputs: Invoice type (achat/vente), amount HT, TVA rate (preset to CEMAC 18% / UEMOA 18% / Morocco 20%), deductibility flag
- Outputs: Correct journal entry with account numbers, TVA settlement calculation, debit/credit balance check

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** The existing Journal Entry playground covers general transactions; this drill focuses on the specific TVA sub-ledger accounts that are uniquely challenging in the OHADA zone and differ from IFRS treatments.

### P4 — Ratio Analysis & DuPont Decomposition
**EN:** Financial Ratio Analysis & DuPont Decomposition
**FR:** Analyse des Ratios Financiers & Décomposition DuPont

**Target learner:** Master 1, practicing professionals

**Pedagogical objective:** Finance professionals need to diagnose *why* ROE changes, not just compute it. The DuPont decomposition (ROE = net margin × asset turnover × equity multiplier) is taught theoretically but rarely practised interactively. This playground makes the algebra visible and connects textbook ratios to business intuition.

**Core interaction:** The learner enters 2 years of condensed IS and BS data. The tool computes a full ratio dashboard (profitability, efficiency, solvency, liquidity) and renders an animated DuPont tree showing each driver's contribution to the ROE change. A peer-comparison mode lets the learner benchmark against preset industry medians for the OHADA region.

**Minimum viable inputs/outputs:**
- Inputs: Revenue, EBITDA, EBIT, net income, total assets, fixed assets, trade receivables, inventory, equity, total debt (year N and N-1)
- Outputs: 12-ratio dashboard, DuPont tree with variance attribution, year-over-year delta arrows, industry-median overlay

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** CVP explains how volume, price, and cost mix drive profit at the managerial level; this playground explains how the resulting profit translates to shareholder returns at the financial-analysis level. Together they bridge managerial and financial accounting perspectives required at Master level.

**EN:** Depreciation Method Comparator
**FR:** Comparateur des Méthodes d'Amortissement

**Target learner:** Licence 3, Master 1, professionals switching between SYSCOHADA and IFRS

A "SYSCOHADA vs IFRS delta" row highlights the timing differences relevant to deferred tax.

**Minimum viable inputs/outputs:**
- Inputs: Asset cost, useful life (years or units), residual value, productive-capacity units, component split (optional)
- Outputs: Multi-curve chart, comparison table, depreciation difference schedule, effective tax-rate impact note

**Why it complements existing playgrounds:** The existing `depreciation-playground.html` is a static HTML file (3 085 lines) with no native integration. This proposal is the native-module migration of that playground, extended with comparative analytics and the component method gap — making it the natural next migration target after `cvp` and `journal-entry`.
